Passage 1: CPC Celebrates 105th Anniversary

The Communist Party of China (CPC) celebrated its 105th founding anniversary on July 1, 2026. A grand ceremony was held at the Great Hall of the People in Beijing, where President Xi Jinping presented the "July 1st Medal" to outstanding Party members and delivered an important speech.

In his speech, President Xi reviewed the Party's glorious history and called on all Party members to "strive to create new historical glory." He emphasized the importance of maintaining the Party's original aspirations and mission, and continuing to serve the people wholeheartedly.

The Central Archives also released over 44,000 historical documents to the public on this special occasion, showcasing the Party's journey and achievements over the past century. A special concert titled "People First" was broadcast on the evening of July 1st to celebrate this milestone.

Foreign political parties and leaders from various countries sent congratulatory messages, highly praising the CPC's governance achievements and noting that China's development provides opportunities and references for the world, especially for the Global South.

Passage 2: Typhoon Warning and Flood Season

China entered its main flood season on July 1, 2026, according to the Ministry of Water Resources. During the main flood season from July to August, multiple rainy areas are expected in both northern and southern China, with northern regions facing heavier flooding. Local extreme rainstorms and floods are predicted to be more frequent, and stronger typhoons may move northward to affect inland areas.

The Central Meteorological Observatory issued a blue typhoon warning at 6 PM on July 1st. A tropical depression in the South China Sea is expected to move west-northwest at a speed of 15-20 kilometers per hour, gradually strengthening into a typhoon within 24 hours. It is forecast to make landfall along the eastern coast of Hainan Island to western Guangdong on the afternoon or evening of July 3rd.

The Qiongzhou Strait passenger ferry service was suspended starting from 3 PM on July 2nd due to the tropical depression, with the suspension expected to last 2-3 days. Ticket sales at various ports were halted from 1 PM on July 2nd. Passengers and drivers needing to cross the strait were advised to pay close attention to typhoon updates.

Meanwhile, a blue rainstorm warning was also issued. Heavy to torrential rain is expected in parts of Anhui, Jiangxi, Zhejiang, Hubei, Hunan, Chongqing, Guizhou, Sichuan, Yunnan, Guangxi, and Hainan Island from 8 PM on July 1st to 8 PM on July 2nd.
